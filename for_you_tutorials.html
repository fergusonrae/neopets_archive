» Packrat

Getting started
If you're reading this, then you probably are seeking the amazing packrat avatar.


The packrat avatar is an avatar that will probably take you at least a week to get, but getting there is half the fun!


How to get it
To get the packrat avatar, you have to look at your Safety Deposit Box (SDB) when it has 1,000 different items in it. Not 1,000 items all together, but 1,000 different items. You can see how many items your safety deposit box has by going to it and looking at the top. You should see something that says Items: xxx | Qty: xxx where the xxx is the number. Items is how many different items you have, while Qty is how many items you have all together.

That's a lot of items, and it can be kind of hard finding so many items to put in your SDB for cheap. So, For You has made a list of 1,000 items for 150nps or less to start filling up your SDB. Scroll down a bit more and you'll find it.

Shop Boards
There's another way to get Neopian items for cheap though. It's name is 1np sales. If you are over 13, you can visit the NeoBoards. If you visit the Shop Ads Board, you'll see a bunch of threads posting things like "Visit my cheap shop!" and "I need a ____ for cheap!" But, there are also occasionally boards saying things like 1np Sale! Those are what you are here for.

1np Sales are when a user is feeling generous so they will, after a certain number of people post on their board, post a time where, in their shop, there will be a number of items for 1np that usually sell for more. It's usually items that are worth 30-50 neopoints they are putting up, But sometimes there will be rare, even pricey items they are giving away.

What's so great about these sales is that you are getting practically free items you can resell or use and you can get items for your SDB collection for very cheap. However, many people use these sales, so in order to get the items the generous user is practically giving away, you need to be quick.

Before the user even posts the time for the next sale, have their shop open on another tab so you don't go searching for the link. Next, when they do post the time, go over to their shop 1 minute early and begin refreshing. Sometimes the user will start the sale a little early, giving you a chance to get some items before other people have gotten to the shop. And lastly, have fun. Chat with the other people on that board and get to know the person hosting the sale. Make friends and time will go by quicker between sales.

Da List
Now for the list. The list of 1,000 items that you can buy for cheap to over stuff your SDB. but remember, this list is a jumping off point. If there are other items you want to use for Packrat, go ahead and use them.

I'd also like to mention that while some words have underscores at the end of them, like onion_ you do not include the underscore when searching it on the shop wizard, trades, auctions, or however it is that you are getting these items. That underscore is put in automatically by TNT, just so you know :)

And, with this enormous list, I leave you. Good luck in your search for the SDB - Packrat avatar!

» Saving Gimp Brushes

Ooh!
So, you found For You's guide to saving gimp brushes. No, that doesn't mean you'll be saving gimp brushes from harm like Superman or Wonderwoman, because I know that's what you thought this tutorial was about ;) But it means to save images in Gimp, so you can use them as brushes later on.

Why do I need brushes?
Brushes simply add pizazz to otherwise plain pictures. They make layouts and graphics look more interesting, and professional. Here's a little glimpse of what a couple of simple brushes can do to a picture.






Which one?
Wasn't the second image more beautiful? I personally thought it was, and if you want your images to look like that, brushes are a must. On the second image, I used three distinct brushes. That's all. So, now that you know that images can enhance your images, on to the tutorial.

Finding your brushes
You do not need to download brushes from other sites to make brushes. All you need is your program of Gimp, and an image, preferably black-and-white. There are certain websites you can go to, though for images that are great for brushes. These sites have pages of images, made just so you can use them on your graphics. Here are some marvelous, Neopets sites that have great brushes.

Arie's Stamps
Chelsea's Stamps
Jay and Erin's Resource Page
Luna's Stamps
Mary's Stamps

Stamps?
If you noticed that the above links say that they are actually giving you stamps, and got a little confused, you're not alone. Stamps and brushes are very, very similar. Brushes are used like pens or pencils. You can drag them across the page, and they'll make one continuous line. Stamps are used like stamps. You do not drag stamps across your page, you just press down and release.

But here's where it gets even trickier. Brushes can be used as stamps. IE, you can turn a stamp into a brush, and it will work just fine. So, imagine clicking on your brush tool, and selecting a normal circle as your brush. You could either drag that brush across the page, or you could just click once, and leave a perfect circle behind. So, what I'm saying is, that even though those sites say they are stamps, and they are, you can save them as brushes, without any worries ;D

Copying your brush
For this tutorial, I am going to be using one of the brushes from Arie's Stamps (link above) just so you can see how to do it with the pages above. You go to the site, and then scroll down until you find a stamp you like. Then right click on it, and choose Copy Image.



Pasting and selecting your brush
Now that you have copied your brush, open your Gimp program, and go to Edit - Paste. If you used one of the sites above, you'll see that the stamp you wanted wasn't the only thing you just pasted. Lots of other stamps cam along for the ride. So, choose your lasso tool, and outline the stamp that you want to use. When you're finished choose Edit - Copy, then Edit - Paste as - New image.







Now, the stamp you selected should be in another window of Gimp. Go along the edges of the image and erase all the little stray pixels that aren't a part of your stamp, if you have any. Then, go up to Layer - Transparency - Color to Alpha. A little screen will the pop up, showing your image. Make sure that the color you have selected is white, and view your image. There should be no white spaces, and it should look very crisp and clear. If it does, push okay.





Almost done!
Now, your actual image should look just like it did in the preview. Go up to Image - Mode - Grayscale. This makes your image into black-and-white, which it should be already. But, you can't save it as an image if it's not in that mode.

Now, you may choose select - all and then Edit - copy. I trust that you don't need a screenie for that :)



Saving your brush
Now that you've copied your brush, go to Edit - Paste As - New Brush... Another screen will pop up. Rewrite the Brush Name whatever you wish. I will be naming mine Line-Dots because it looks like a line of dots to me, and when I am looking for that brush, it will be easier to find. Leave the file name alone. It should say mybrush, do NOT change that, and leave the spacing at 25px. Trust me, that's where you want it to be. ^^. Then choose OK to save your brush!



Yes, done!
Congratulations, you've created a brush! That wasn't so hard, was it? Now, if you want to learn how to find your brush, stay tuned.

To find your brush, click on the Paintbrush Tool, to start. You will find, later on, that your brush can be used on many other tools, but the Paint Brush tool is the most basic, and easy to understand. When you click on it, below, some new text should appear. Next to the word Brush, there should be a box. Click on it, and find your brush.





What else?
After choosing your brush, you can also then choose the opacity and scale. the opacity is how see-through your brush is. If the opacity is at 100%, then you can not see any of the background behind it. If it is at 0%, then the image is clear. Try out this setting for further explanation. the scale is how big the brush is. If the scale is at exactly 1, then your brush will be the same size as when you copy and pasted it when you created it. If the number is less than 1, then it is smaller, more than one and it is larger. Also try out this setting.

Experiment Time!
Now, the choice is up to you. You can create more brushes if you like, or you could try them out, or you could close down Gimp and forget you ever made a brush. But, I have to admit, playing with the brushes is fun. If you know how to layer images, you can have a blast with brushes. Changing their color also changes the feeling of the picture. Have white, snowflake-like brushes in the corner of your image, and you automatically think "winter" or "snow".

Once you get the hang of brushes, you'll wonder what you ever did without them. Until then, look at other people's graphics to see how they used their brushes. (You will also notice that the difference between good graphics and amazing graphics is the brush work) and study it. Never steal another person's image, but look at how they layered and color schemed their brushes.
